## It means the document is drafted before you sit down to write it

The billable-hour maths of documentation is unkind. A twenty-minute client meeting can generate an hour of writing up: the attendance note, the file note, the letter confirming advice. **The thinking was done in the room. The rest is formatting.**

What changes here is the starting point. You dictate the matter, attach whatever came with it — the executed agreement, photographs, correspondence — and what comes back is not a transcript but **a structured document, already in your template, with the facts under the right headings.**

That is a different task to face. Editing a draft that is eighty per cent right is faster and less mentally taxing than starting at a blank page at 6pm. The time saving is real, but the more useful shift is that document production stops being the thing you dread at the end of the day.

## It means small firms can produce structured documents without adding staff

This is the part with the clearest commercial implication, and it is worth being precise about it.

> For a sole practitioner or a small firm that has never had a typist, this removes the reason to engage one. For a firm with staff already, nothing is taken away — their work shifts from typing to checking.

For a sole practitioner or a small firm that has never had a typist, the honest position is that this removes the reason to engage one. Producing a properly formatted attendance note or letter of advice no longer requires either your own time reformatting or an outsourced transcription cost. **You dictate, review, sign off.**

For firms that do have support staff, nothing is taken away. The assistant sits inside the existing SpeechLive author-and-typist workflow — the AI produces the first draft, and your assistant or PA reviews and finalises it, with the audio, transcript and draft held together as one job. The staff member's work becomes **checking and refining rather than typing from scratch.**

The distinction matters: this is about not needing to add headcount as volume grows, not about replacing people already doing the work.

## It means the file stays in one place, under your control

Documentation tends to fragment. Audio in one app, a transcript emailed to someone, a draft in a shared folder, the final version somewhere in the practice management system, and three people unsure which one is current.

Keeping the recording, the transcript, the AI draft and the review in a single workflow removes most of that. **There is one version of the matter's documentation and one place it lives.** For a practice manager, that is a supervision and file-integrity question as much as an efficiency one — you can see what stage each document is at without asking.

It also matters that **nothing is finalised automatically.** Every output is prepared for a person to check and approve. The professional judgement, and the responsibility that goes with it, stays exactly where it has always been.

## It means client confidentiality is answerable, not assumed

For legal work this is often the deciding factor rather than a footnote.

The data is **hosted in Australia** — it stays in-region and is not moved outside the designated storage location. AI processing runs in an isolated Microsoft Azure environment and is **deleted once processing is complete.** The material is **not shared with public AI services and is not used to train AI models.** The underlying platform is certified to **ISO 27001** and **ISO 9001**, is **GDPR-compliant**, and is built to meet **EU AI Act** requirements.

The practical meaning of that list is that when a client, an insurer or your own risk committee asks where confidential material goes, there is a documented answer rather than a reassurance. Firms that have avoided AI tools on confidentiality grounds — reasonably — have a different conversation to have here.
